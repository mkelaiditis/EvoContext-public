# Veridian Pro Payment Processing and Timelines

This document explains payment processing timelines for Veridian Pro, including how approved refunds are handled. It focuses on the operational timing of payment processing and does not describe eligibility criteria or the conditions for approval. The intent is to help customers understand how long financial transactions may take after approval.

Approved refunds are processed within 5–10 business days. This timeline reflects the time required to submit the refund through payment networks and for the funds to appear in the original payment method. The exact timing depends on the payment method, the issuing bank, and regional processing rules. Veridian Software cannot accelerate bank processing times once a refund has been submitted to the payment network.

Customers may receive a confirmation notice when a refund is initiated. This notice indicates that the refund has been submitted for processing and includes a reference number. The appearance of funds in an account may still take several business days after this notice is issued. Customers who do not see the funds after the stated window should contact their payment provider first and then contact Veridian Software support if further assistance is needed.

Payment processing timelines for other transactions, such as charge captures or payment method updates, may vary based on the payment provider. Some payment methods process faster than others, and international transactions can introduce additional delays. Veridian Software provides the processing date and transaction reference to help customers reconcile account statements.

In some cases, a payment provider may place a temporary hold or perform additional checks. These checks are outside of Veridian Software's control and are managed by the provider. If a provider requests additional verification, the customer may be contacted by the provider directly. Veridian Software will provide any necessary transaction details to assist with verification if requested by the customer.

Customers should also consider bank holidays and regional settlement schedules when reviewing timelines. A transaction submitted late in a business day may be processed on the following business day, and cross-border payments may be routed through intermediary networks that add extra processing steps. These operational factors can influence when funds appear, even when Veridian Software has completed its portion of the transaction.

For organizational accounts, finance teams may use the transaction reference to match internal records. The reference is included in the payment notification and can also be retrieved from the customer portal. The portal provides a centralized history of transactions to help administrators manage their financial records.

This document is limited to timelines and operational details for payment processing. It does not describe qualification criteria, plan-specific conditions, or account procedures. For questions about eligibility or account changes, customers should consult the relevant policy documents or contact support for guidance.
