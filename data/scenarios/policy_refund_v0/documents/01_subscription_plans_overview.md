# Veridian Pro Subscription Plans Overview

Veridian Software offers Veridian Pro in three primary plans designed for different operational needs: Monthly, Annual, and Enterprise. This overview explains commitment lengths, pricing tiers, and the feature scope of each plan so organizations can choose the right fit. The plans are built on the same core platform, and differences focus on usage allowances, administrative capabilities, and access to advanced modules.

The Monthly plan is intended for individuals and small teams that prefer a short commitment. It renews on a monthly cadence and provides immediate access to the core workspace, task automation, and analytics dashboards. The Monthly plan includes a baseline storage allocation, standard integration connectors, and shared project libraries. It is designed to be flexible for teams that expect shifting workloads or shorter project horizons.

The Annual plan provides access to the same core platform with a longer commitment length and a structured pricing model. Annual tiers are organized by seat count and usage bands, allowing teams to scale predictably. The Annual plan includes all standard integrations plus expanded audit logs and longer activity history. It is commonly selected by departments that want stable budgeting, multi-quarter initiatives, and uniform feature access across teams.

The Enterprise plan is designed for large organizations with specialized compliance or deployment needs. It includes dedicated account management, custom security review support, and optional private networking features. Enterprise pricing is structured around a base platform fee plus usage-based components, with the exact tiering finalized during onboarding. This plan also offers advanced governance features, including organization-wide policy controls, delegated administration, and extended retention options.

Across all plans, Veridian Pro includes role-based access, configurable workflows, and searchable knowledge spaces. The platform supports integrations with popular identity providers, source repositories, and ticketing systems. All plans include service monitoring, usage analytics, and a consistent interface to minimize training overhead as teams grow. Feature availability is published in the Veridian Pro product matrix, which is updated as new modules are released.

Pricing tiers within each plan are based on seat ranges and usage levels, such as monthly active projects, automation runs, and storage capacity. Entry tiers are designed for small teams, mid tiers address department-level needs, and upper tiers support organization-wide deployments. These tiers ensure that customers can scale usage without switching plan types prematurely. Veridian Software provides a plan selector tool to help estimate which tier aligns with expected usage.

Plan selection should consider commitment length, expected growth, and governance needs. The Monthly plan emphasizes flexibility, the Annual plan emphasizes predictable budgeting and feature continuity, and the Enterprise plan emphasizes control and tailored support. Veridian Software periodically reviews plan packaging to keep features aligned with customer needs, and plan changes are communicated through official product updates.

This overview is intended to explain plan structure at a high level. Detailed feature lists and current pricing are available in the Veridian Pro product catalog and within the customer portal. Customers can review their plan details, seat allocations, and usage limits directly in the portal at any time.
