# Veridian Pro Early Termination and Service Commitment Term

This document describes how Veridian Software handles early termination during a service commitment term for Veridian Pro. It explains how the contract year is defined and how a prorated reimbursement is calculated for unused service value when a customer ends service before the contract year concludes. It does not describe account procedures or payment adjustments.

A service commitment term refers to the agreed period during which the customer maintains access to Veridian Pro under a fixed term arrangement. The contract year begins on the effective start date stated in the purchase confirmation. Early termination occurs when the customer ends access before the contract year is complete.

When early termination occurs, the customer may receive a prorated reimbursement of unused service value. The reimbursement is calculated on a monthly basis using the contract year duration and the remaining full months after termination. Promotional credits are excluded from the calculation and do not increase the unused service value. The calculation considers only the paid portion of the service commitment term and any applicable plan-level usage adjustments.

The unused service value represents the portion of the contract year that remains after early termination. Veridian Software determines the unused service value using the customer’s contract start date, the remaining full months in the service commitment term, and the paid amount associated with those months. This amount is the basis for the prorated reimbursement and is summarized in the account records for the customer.

The company may require confirmation of the effective end date to ensure the prorated reimbursement is calculated accurately. If the effective end date differs from the requested termination date, the reimbursement will reflect the verified date. Veridian Software provides a summary of the calculation upon request so customers can review how the unused service value was determined.

For clarity, the service commitment term is tracked against the contract year start date, and the calculation uses only full months remaining after early termination. Partial months are handled consistently in the monthly proration schedule, and the unused service value reflects only the paid period within the contract year. This approach keeps the prorated reimbursement aligned with the service commitment term and prevents promotional credits from inflating the unused service value.

This document focuses on the service commitment term and the contract year calculations used for early termination. It does not describe purchase eligibility, payment processing timelines, or account change procedures. For any questions about account access or administrative steps, customers should consult the appropriate account documentation or contact support.
