# Veridian Pro Refund Terms and Eligibility

This document summarizes the general refund terms for Veridian Pro, a product of Veridian Software. It is intended to provide a clear, plain-language summary of the refund conditions that apply to standard purchases. These terms apply to new purchases made directly through Veridian Software and to transactions processed through authorized resellers where Veridian Software is the merchant of record.

Customers may request a full refund within 14 days of purchase. This cooling-off window is intended to give new customers time to evaluate whether Veridian Pro meets their needs. Requests must be submitted during the window to be considered, and the request date must fall within the 14-day period. When a full refund is granted under this window, access to the related service may be adjusted to align with the refund outcome.

Refund eligibility depends on plan type. Veridian Software offers multiple plans with different terms and feature scope, and eligibility is evaluated according to the plan associated with the purchase. This document does not describe the specific eligibility rules for each plan. Customers should consult their plan documentation and purchase confirmation for plan-specific details.

Refund requests should include the account name, the purchase reference, and a brief reason for the request. The request is used to validate the original purchase and to ensure that the request is linked to the correct account. If supporting information is missing, Veridian Software may request additional details before a request can be evaluated.

Refunds are granted only for qualifying purchases. Requests that fall outside the cooling-off window may be denied, and Veridian Software may decline requests that are inconsistent with the stated terms. The company may also decline requests where an account has been involved in suspected misuse or policy violations. These decisions are made to keep the refund process consistent for all customers.

If a request is approved, the refund will be issued to the original payment instrument whenever possible. The customer will receive confirmation that the refund has been authorized by Veridian Software. If a payment instrument is no longer valid, Veridian Software will provide guidance on alternate methods, subject to verification of ownership and identity.

These general terms are intended to clarify the availability of refunds within the initial evaluation window and the high-level eligibility principles. They do not replace the full purchase terms or any plan-specific terms that may apply. For more information about plan eligibility, customers should consult their plan documentation and the purchase confirmation delivered at the time of checkout.
